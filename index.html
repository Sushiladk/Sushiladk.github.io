About

Welcome to my website! I am a graduate student of physics at National Tsing Hua University in Taiwan. Originally from Nepal, I moved to Taiwan in 2021 to pursue my studies and explore new opportunities. My current area of research includes parts of atomic physics and quantum optics, with a focus on the intriguing phenomenon of Electromagnetically Induced Transparency (EIT) and its derivatives under supervision of Prof. Ite. A. Yu at Ultracold Lab . My work involves theoretical aspects, running simulations, and developing models based on these results. When I'm not immersed in the world of physics, I love to travel and explore new places, play sports, and watch movies. I'm also passionate about learning programming, and always eager to improve my skills in this area. In addition to my academic pursuits, I have a deep interest in world geography and history. I enjoy reading and learning about different cultures and traditions, and how they have shaped the world we live in today. On this website, you'll find information about my research, as well as my other interests and hobbies. Please feel free to get in touch if you have any questions. Thank you for visiting!

Skills

My skills in mathematics, physics, and programming have been developed through various experiences in academic and research settings. I have used Mathematica for both symbolic and numerical computations, making use of various functionalities including differential equation solving, Fourier analysis, and matrix manipulations. These skills have allowed me to conduct simulations for quantum systems dynamics and investigate the behavior of different energy level atomic system. I also have experience in programming with Python and have worked on my undergraduate project on the computational study of chaos in the damped driven pendulum. In addition, I have experience with Latex, Qiskit, Bash on Ubuntu.

Theoretical Study of Cross Phase Modulation in Double Rydberg EIT Slow Light Scheme

Theoretical Study on Trapping Potential for Rydberg polaritons based on Electromagnetically induced transparency to achieve BEC

National Tsing Hua University | 2021 - Present

Supervisor: Prof. Ite A. Yu

  • Using the combined effect of Electromagnetically induced transparency and strong DDI interaction of Rydberg atom, theoretical study on cross phase modulation in Double Rydberg slow light scheme is investigated.
  • Rydberg polaritons being a 3D quasi-bosonic particle, we study role and geometry of trapping potential for undergoing Bose-Einstein Condensation.
